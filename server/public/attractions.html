Statue of Liberty

The Statue of Liberty, a gift from France to the United States, is one of the most recognizable symbols of freedom and democracy in the world. Standing on Liberty Island in New York Harbor, this colossal neoclassical sculpture was designed by Frédéric Auguste Bartholdi and dedicated in 1886. Visitors can explore the pedestal and even climb to the crown for panoramic views of New York City and the harbor.

Times Square

Known as "The Crossroads of the World," Times Square is one of the most iconic and vibrant locations in New York City. Famous for its bright, neon lights and towering digital billboards, it’s a bustling commercial and entertainment hub. Times Square draws millions of visitors annually, offering everything from Broadway shows to world-class shopping, dining, and the famous New Year's Eve ball drop.

Hudson Yards Vessel

The Vessel, located in the Hudson Yards development, is a striking architectural landmark that opened to the public in 2019. This interactive art piece consists of a honeycomb-like structure of connected staircases, offering unique perspectives of the city and the Hudson River. Visitors can climb its 154 flights of stairs to enjoy one-of-a-kind views of Manhattan’s west side and the surrounding skyline.
